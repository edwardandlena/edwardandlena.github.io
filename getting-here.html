By sea

The closest port of call to Santa Cruz is the Santa Cruz small craft harbor, located south of Santa Cruz in Monterey Bay. Please view their website for information such as current channel soundings and visitor berthing procedures.

By train

Amtrak serves downtown Santa Cruz (station code SCZ) by bus connection from the nearest train station, San Jose Diridon (SJC). Amtrak routes that stop in San Jose include the Coast Starlight (serving Seattle, Portland, and Los Angeles) and the Capitol Corridor (Oakland, Berkeley, and Sacramento). You can buy a ticket directly to Santa Cruz, and once you disembark from the train in San Jose, proceed to the 17 Express bus stop.

Regional trains stopping at San Jose Diridon include the Caltrain from San Francisco and the peninsula, and the Altamont Corridor Express from Dublin/Pleasanton and Stockton.

By bus

Santa Cruz Metro buses can get you into town from all over Santa Cruz county. Route 17 runs between San Jose Diridon and downtown Santa Cruz approximately once per hour, and requires exact change of $7 to board.

Greyhound buses also come to downtown Santa Cruz.

By air

Norman Y. Mineta San Jose International Airport (SJC) is the closest airport to Santa Cruz, about a 45-minute drive, and is connected to San Jose Diridon via bus and light rail. San Francisco (SFO) and Oakland (OAK) International Airports are both a little more than an hour's drive, and are near Caltrain and Amtrak Capitol Corridor stations, respectively.

By car

Santa Cruz is the southern terminus of California State Highway 17. For a scenic coastal alternative, California State Highway 1 passes through the city as well.
